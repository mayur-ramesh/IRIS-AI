# Seasonal Signals


## July First Trading Day Powerhouse [weekly_timing]

First trading day in July: S&P up 32 of last 36 (89%), avg gain 0.5%. One of the single strongest trading days in the calendar year. Driven by 2nd-half retirement fund inflows.

## July Front-Loaded Strength [macro_context]

July strength concentrated in first half. Graph shows gains front-loaded then fading. Huge gain in July usually provides better buying opportunity over next 4 months. NASDAQ enters worst 4 months (Jul-Oct).

## Midterm July Split Personality [macro_context]

Midterm July: Dow +1.6% (#3), S&P +1.3% (#3), but NASDAQ -0.8% (#7), Russell 2K -2.5%. Large-caps hold up while small-caps and tech suffer. IRIS should note this divergence for SPY vs broader market.

## July OpEx Day Bearish [weekly_timing]

July OpEx day: Dow down 24 of 43, avg -0.20%. S&P avg -0.23%. NASDAQ avg -0.35%, down 27 of 45. NASDAQ OpEx week D7 streak (down 7 straight years).

## Post-July-OpEx Volatile [weekly_timing]

Week after July OpEx prone to big swings: Dow up 17 of 23 but with massive individual moves (+3-4% and -3-4%). High volatility zone.

## Super-8 Days Pattern [weekly_timing]

Market gains more on Super-8 days (last 3 + first 2 + middle 3 trading days) than all 13 remaining days combined. S&P Super-8 avg 0.56% per period vs 0.03% rest of month. 401(k) inflows drive mid-month strength.

## First Month of Quarter Bullish [macro_context]

First month of Q1-Q3 (Jan/Apr/Jul) produces greatest gains historically. S&P Q3 first month avg +1.3%. Institutional annual/semi-annual/quarterly operations drive inflows.
