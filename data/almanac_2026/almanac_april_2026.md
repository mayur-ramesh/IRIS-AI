# April 2026 Almanac Overview

April is the #2 Dow month (avg +1.8%) since 1950. Up 16 straight years from 2006 to 2021. April 2020 Dow gained 11.1%, best April since 1938. Stocks anticipate great Q1 earnings by rising sharply before earnings are reported rather than after. The "Best Six Months" of the year end with April. Recent exceptions where April was dangerous: 2002, 2004, 2005, 2022, 2024, and 2025. In midterm election years since 1950: Dow +0.4%, S&P -0.3%, NASDAQ -1.1%. End of April NASDAQ strength fading.

# Seasonal Signals and Trading Indicators


## Best Six Months Ends [timing]

The 'Best Six Months' (Nov-Apr) ends in April. Start looking for MACD SELL signal on April 1 for Dow and S&P. When triggered, switch equity exposure to Treasury bonds or defensive positions for May-October.

## 4-Year Cycle Sweet Spot Setup [macro_context]

Q2-Q3 of midterm year 2026 is historically the weakest period: Dow -2.0%, S&P -2.5%, NASDAQ -6.6% avg. But this sets up the Sweet Spot from Q4 2026 to Q2 2027 where Dow gains 19.3%, S&P +20.2%, NASDAQ +29.4%. Look for late Q3/early Q4 low (Aug-Oct) as buying opportunity.

## April OpEx Week Historically Strong [weekly_timing]

April options expiration week: Dow up 35 of 46 (76%), avg +0.89%. S&P up 30 of 46, avg +0.68%. Week AFTER opex also strong: S&P up 30 of 46, avg +0.49%. Current OpEx week streak D4 for S&P (down last 4 years).

## Post-Tax-Deadline Strength [weekly_timing]

April exhibits strength after the tax deadline (Apr 15) in recent years. Earnings season anticipation drives pre-report rallies.

## Midterm Year April Weakness [macro_context]

In midterm election years since 1950, April averages are weaker: Dow +0.4%, S&P -0.3%, NASDAQ -1.1%. 2026 is a midterm year — expect below-average April performance vs all-years average.

## Good Friday Effect [weekly_timing]

NASDAQ up 21 of last 25 days before Good Friday (Apr 3, 2026). Day after Easter (Apr 6) is second worst post-holiday trading day.
