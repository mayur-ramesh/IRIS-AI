# January 2026 Almanac Overview

January Barometer predicts year's course with .733 batting average (84.0% accuracy for major calls). Every down January on the S&P since 1950 preceded a new or extended bear market, flat market, or 10% correction. S&P gains in January's first five days preceded full-year gains 83.3% of the time. November, December, and January constitute the year's best three-month span with 4.4% S&P gain. January NASDAQ powerful at 2.6% avg since 1971. Midterm year Januarys are significantly negative: Dow -0.7%, S&P -0.9%, NASDAQ -0.7%. The January Indicator Trifecta (Santa Claus Rally + First Five Days + January Barometer all positive) has S&P up 90.6% of the time (29 of 32 years), avg gain 17.7%.

# Seasonal Signals and Trading Indicators

## January Barometer [risk_indicator]

As S&P goes in January, so goes the year. 84.0% accuracy since 1950 with only 12 major errors in 75 years. Devised by Yale Hirsch in 1972. Including flat-year errors yields 73.3% accuracy. Full years followed January direction in 11 of last 19 midterm election years.

## First Five Days Early Warning [risk_indicator]

Last 48 up First Five Days followed by full-year gains 83.3% of time, avg 14.2% gain in all 48 years. 27 down First Five Days followed by 15 up and 12 down (44.4% accurate), avg gain 1.1%. Poor record in midterm years: only 9 of 19 followed direction.

## January Indicator Trifecta [risk_indicator]

Combines Santa Claus Rally, First Five Days, and January Barometer. When all three positive: S&P up 90.6% of time (29 of 32 years), avg gain 17.7%, next 11 months up 87.5% (28/32), avg 12.6%. When all three down: S&P avg loss -3.6% with bear markets in 1969, 2000, 2008. When preceded by prior-year bear: always positive (14-0), avg full-year gain 22.2%.

## Best Three-Month Span Ends [timing]

November, December, and January constitute the best three-month span with 4.4% S&P gain. January NASDAQ particularly powerful at 2.6% avg since 1971.

## Midterm January Weakness [macro_context]

Midterm year Januarys are significantly negative: Dow -0.7%, S&P -0.9%, NASDAQ -0.7%, Russell 1K -1.1%, Russell 2K -1.4%. Contrasts sharply with overall January avg of +1.1% S&P. January ranked #1 for NASDAQ overall but drops to negative in midterm years.

## January Effect / Small Cap [timing]

January Effect favors small-cap stocks but now starts in mid-December. Small caps outperform large caps from mid-Dec through early March. Tax-loss selling creates bargains in beaten-down small stocks.
